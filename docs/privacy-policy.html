Privacy Policy

ScoreCalci — Effective date: June 11, 2026

1. Overview

ScoreCalci is a sports scoring and tournament management application. This policy explains what data we collect, how it is stored, and how it is used.

2. Data We Collect

ScoreCalci collects only the data you create within the app:

  • Match records — scores, sport type, date, players/teams you enter.
  • Tournament records — bracket data, round results, and tournament settings you create.

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information.

3. Authentication

ScoreCalci uses anonymous authentication via Firebase Auth. A random, non-identifiable user ID is generated automatically when you first open the app. No registration, login form, or personal credentials are required.

4. How Data Is Stored

Match and tournament data is stored in two places:

  • On-device — using SharedPreferences for offline-first access.
  • Cloud backup — using Google Cloud Firestore, scoped to your anonymous user ID. No other user can read or write your data. Cloud sync requires an internet connection and is used solely to let you back up and restore your data on the same device.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your data with third parties. Your data is stored in Google Firebase infrastructure and governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

6. Data Deletion

You can delete all your match and tournament data from within the app at any time via the Settings screen. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data. Cloud data associated with your anonymous account is deleted within 180 days of inactivity.

7. Children's Privacy

ScoreCalci is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date above and publish the revised version at this URL.
